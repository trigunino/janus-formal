## Question

Can the category of natural operators on decorated SpinC immersions be identified
with a category of equivariant jet representations?

The original slogan was

```text
C_op  ~=  C_jets.
```

## Verdict

The slogan is **false as stated**. The corrected local theorem is:

> **Classical categorical jet theorem — external theorem, categorically
> repackaged.** In fixed base dimension, finite-order natural bundles together
> with regular local natural differential operators of globally finite order are
> equivalent to a category whose objects are finite-order jet-group actions and
> whose morphisms are smooth equivariant maps defined on finite jet
> prolongations. Composition is induced by holonomic jet prolongation.

For the Janus SpinC-immersion problem the corresponding statement is currently
**conditional**:

> **Conditional Janus theorem.** Once the structured jet groupoid, descent
> theorem, natural field bundles, regularity/locality hypotheses and holonomic
> composition are constructed, regular local finite-order Janus operators are
> equivalent to equivariant morphisms over that structured jet groupoid.

The right-hand side is not, in general, the ordinary category of linear
representations of one group.

## 1. Why the naive statement fails

### N1 — representations are too narrow

A finite-order natural **vector** bundle is modeled by a linear representation
of a jet group. A general natural bundle is modeled by a smooth action on a
manifold. Affine bundles of connections are the standard warning.

Thus the object category must contain smooth jet-group actions, and in the
structured Janus case equivariant bundles over a jet base.

### N2 — ordinary equivariant maps see only order zero

An ordinary equivariant map

```text
S -> T
```

represents an order-zero natural transformation. An operator of order `k` is
represented by

```text
T_m^k S -> T,
```

where `T_m^k S` is the standard fiber of the `k`-jet prolongation. The exterior
derivative is the basic example: it is not a map between the unprolonged fibers
of differential forms.

Therefore the morphism sets must contain maps from jet prolongations, with a
variable finite order.

### N3 — composition is not plain composition of fiber maps

If

```text
phi : T_m^k S -> T,
psi : T_m^l T -> U,
```

then the composite is represented by

```text
T_m^(k+l) S
  -> T_m^l (T_m^k S)       holonomic prolongation
  -> T_m^l T               prolong phi
  -> U.                     psi
```

This is the co-Kleisli-type morphism law generated by the jet-prolongation
tower. The Lean gate

```text
P0EFTJanusJetOperatorComposition
  .composite_operator_factors_through_higher_jet
```

formalizes the required factorization in an abstract finite model.

### N4 — naturality alone does not imply locality

On compact Riemannian manifolds,

```text
A_M(g,f)(x) = Vol_g(M)^(-1) * integral_M f dvol_g
```

is natural under diffeomorphisms, but it is nonlocal. Changing `f` away from
`x` changes `A_M(g,f)(x)` while preserving the entire germ of `f` at `x`.

Hence no finite or infinite point jet determines every natural operator.
Locality is indispensable.

### N5 — local jets do not classify global topology

Holonomy, the topological SpinC class, a chosen square-root line, a `Z4` lift,
determinant-line holonomy and spectral projectors are not determined by one
point jet. The categorical theorem therefore classifies the **local differential
layer**, not the whole global Janus theory.

## 2. Correct classical category

Fix a dimension `m`.

### Definition — operator category

Let `NatDiff_m^fo` have:

- finite-order regular natural bundles on `m`-manifolds as objects;
- regular local natural differential operators of one globally finite order as
  morphisms.

The globally finite-order restriction can later be replaced by a pro-jet or
locally cylindrical formalism, but it is required for the finite category below.

### Definition — jet-operator category

An object is represented by a smooth action

```text
rho : G_m^r x S -> S
```

of a finite jet group. Actions are identified after inflation to a higher jet
order.

For objects `S,T`, define morphisms schematically by

```text
Hom_JetOp(S,T)
  = colim_(k,q) C-infinity(T_m^k S,T)^(G_m^q),
```

where `q` is high enough to act on the source jet and target. Transition maps in
`k` are induced by jet truncation. Composition is the holonomic formula from
N3.

This is a category of jet-prolonged equivariant morphisms, not the ordinary
representation category `Rep(G_m^r)`.

## 3. Classical equivalence

### Theorem C1 — external theorem, categorical reformulation

There is an equivalence

```text
NatDiff_m^fo  ~=  JetOp_m.
```

### Proof decomposition

#### C1-E1 — construction

For a natural bundle `F` of order `r`, take its standard fiber

```text
S = F_0(R^m).
```

Naturality gives the `G_m^r` action. For an order-`k` operator `D`, define

```text
phi_D(j_0^k s) = D_Rm(s)(0).
```

Finite order makes this well-defined; naturality makes it equivariant.

#### C1-E2 — faithfulness

If two operators have the same standard-fiber evaluator, choose a chart at an
arbitrary point. Naturality transports both operators to the same evaluator on
the standard jet fiber, so their values agree at every point.

#### C1-E3 — fullness

Given an equivariant map

```text
phi : T_m^k S -> T,
```

use the associated-bundle presentations

```text
J^k F(M) ~= P^q M x_(G_m^q) T_m^k S,
G(M)     ~= P^q M x_(G_m^q) T.
```

Equivariance makes

```text
[u,xi] |-> [u,phi(xi)]
```

well-defined. Composing with `j^k s` reconstructs a natural operator.

#### C1-E4 — essential surjectivity

A smooth `G_m^r` action on `S` defines the associated natural bundle

```text
P^r M x_(G_m^r) S.
```

Conversely, every finite-order natural bundle has this associated-bundle form.

### Evidence

This theorem is not proved in Lean here. It is a categorical repackaging of the
classification in Kolář--Michor--Slovák, especially Chapter IV, Section 14:
finite-order natural operators are canonically identified with smooth
equivariant maps between the corresponding standard jet fibers.

## 4. Gauge-natural version

### Theorem C2 — external theorem

For principal `G`-bundles, replace `G_m^r` by the principal prolongation group

```text
W_m^r G.
```

Regular gauge-natural bundles of order `r` are associated to `W^r P`, and
finite-order gauge-natural operators are classified by the corresponding smooth
equivariant jet maps.

This covers a fixed SpinC principal bundle and gauge data only after the exact
category and compatible actions have been specified. It does not automatically
cover a variable immersion, variable ambient geometry or global lift choices.

## 5. Structured Janus jets require a groupoid

Let `B_R` denote the space of admissible `R`-jets of the background data:

```text
immersion,
ambient and induced metrics,
SpinC data and connections,
normal data,
twist lines,
PT and BRST decorations,
chosen square-root/Z4 data when present.
```

Coordinate, frame and gauge changes form a structured jet groupoid

```text
Gamma_R  ==>  B_R.
```

A natural field sector is then modeled by a `Gamma_R`-equivariant bundle over
`B_R`, not merely by one representation on one fixed vector space. Curvature,
second fundamental form, twist curvature and their derivatives distinguish
orbits; isotropy groups can change between strata.

### Theorem C3 — conditional

Assume:

1. finite-dimensional differentiable groupoids `Gamma_R ==> B_R` are
   constructed;
2. natural field bundles satisfy effective descent from equivariant bundles on
   those groupoids;
3. operators are regular, local and of globally finite order on the chosen
   configuration region;
4. global SpinC, root and holonomy choices are retained separately rather than
   reconstructed from point jets.

Then

```text
NatDiff_loc^fo(Janus)  ~=  JetOp(Gamma_bullet).
```

The proof of faithfulness and fullness is the same descent argument as C1. The
substantive Janus work lies in constructing `Gamma_R`, proving descent, and
identifying the admissible reduced jet space.

independently.

## 7. Peetre--Slovák scope

### Theorem C4 — external analytic theorem

A regular morphism of sheaves of smooth sections is locally a differential
operator: near each infinite jet it factors through some finite jet space.

This is local finite order. It does not imply one global order on an arbitrary
unbounded or disconnected configuration space.

The repository already contains an explicit componentwise finite-order model
with no uniform global bound. Consequently there are two honest frameworks:

```text
finite-order category,
```

or

```text
infinite-jet/pro-object category with locally cylindrical morphisms.
```

The present categorical theorem uses the first.

## 8. Finite generation is a separate theorem

The statement

```text
equivariant maps on a finite-dimensional jet space
  -> finitely generated family of invariants
```

is false without additional hypotheses.

- Smooth equivariant maps form infinite-dimensional spaces.
- Polynomial invariant rings need not be finitely generated for arbitrary
  nonreductive actions; jet groups have nonreductive higher-order parts.
- After a geometric normal-form theorem reduces the problem to a compact
  residual group acting linearly, Hilbert finite generation and the Schwarz
  theorem become available for scalar invariants.

Therefore the viable theorem queue is:

```text
structured jet normalization
  -> compact/reductive residual action when available
  -> polynomial invariant generators
  -> smooth invariant/equivariant module theorem
  -> extension across isotropy strata.
```

Pseudo-Riemannian signatures require separate treatment because the residual
orthogonal group is noncompact.

## 9. Root and Z4 data are inputs, not automatic functors

### Lemma C5 — demonstrated obstruction

There is no continuous natural choice assigning to every Hermitian line `L` a
line `R(L)` with a natural isomorphism

```text
R(L) tensor R(L) ~= L.
```

On a point, such a choice would induce a continuous homomorphism

```text
s : U(1) -> U(1),
s(z)^2 = z.
```

On fundamental groups this would require an integer `a` with `2a=1`, which is
impossible. A square-root line must therefore be supplied as extra structure.

### Lemma C6 — demonstrated obstruction

The projection

```text
Z4 -> Z2
```

has no group-homomorphic section: the nonzero element of `Z2` has order two,
whereas every odd lift in `Z4` has order four. A `Z4` lift may exist for a
specific global object, but it is neither automatic nor canonically selected.

## 10. Consequences for Program P

### P-D

At a fixed structured jet `b`, pointwise pairings are controlled by

```text
Hom_(H_b)(E_i,b tensor E_j,b, R),
```

where `H_b` is the isotropy group. Global natural pairings are equivariant
sections over `B_R`; they form a module over the algebra of invariant scalar
functions. Pointwise dimension one therefore does not imply one constant global
coupling.

### P-C

Under the categorical equivalence, an Euler operator, its linearization and its
formal-adjoint condition become equivariant jet equations. This is useful, but it
does not remove variational-bicomplex cohomology, boundary/null Lagrangians or
global obstructions to a primitive action.

### Principal symbols

The principal symbol at `b` lies in

```text
Hom_(H_b)(Sym^k T* tensor E_b, F_b).
```

Multiplicity one can make the leading symbol unique up to scale on one stratum.
Lower-order invariant terms and invariant scalar coefficients remain independent.

3. Prove effective descent for the declared tensor, spinor, twist and ghost

4. Formalize the jet tower as a category/comonadic or co-Kleisli structure.
5. Prove a structured jet isomorphism theorem reducing jets to curvature,

6. Stratify by isotropy and prove smooth extension of equivariants across

7. Only then classify scalar invariants, covariants, pairings, Euler families and
